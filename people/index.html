Organisers

Organisational Members

Israel Mason-Williams



Israel Mason-Williams is a PhD candidate at the UKRI’s Safe and Trusted AI Centre of Doctoral Training, he studied for his M.Phil at the University of Cambridge and Bachelors in Computer Science at Queen Mary University of London. He has published fundamental AI research at world class conferences, ICLR and NeurIPS. Israel is committed to providing research that aids understanding of AI systems. He is also engaged with AI policy, as a Talos Fellow, he worked with a multi-disciplinary team of AI enthusiasts to learn about the EU AI Act and the EU’s perspective on AI. Israel strives to make AI robust, fair and inclusive to get the best out of AI for all.

Georgina Cole




Georgina Cole is a multi-disciplinary designer, innovator and creative. She has studied at renowned universities namely;Central Saint Martins, Queen Mary University of London and the Royal College of Art. She has experience with futures consultancy, HACKMASTERS, working on numerous projects including Speculative Futures, EY Future of Consumer Products and Retail Industries and Agentic AI Hackathon. With a keen eye for logistics and event organisation Georgina is in charge of the communication, organisation and outreach of U-AI.

Gabryel Mason-Williams




Gabryel Mason-Williams is a PhD student in AI at Queen Mary University of London, where he runs and organsises the fundamentals of AI reading group. He was the 2023 recipient of the Google Europe Scholarship for Students with Disabilities for his contributions to AI and HPC research while being a neurodivergent role model. He has worked in AI for many years and has been worked at in Diamond Light Source and the Rosalind Franklin Institute to help them meet their High-Performance-Computing endeavours. Gabryel has also authored publications at world class venues ICLR and NeurIPS and hopes that his research can unveil the black box of neural networks.
